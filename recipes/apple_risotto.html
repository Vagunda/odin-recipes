Apple Risotto

Description

The dish is a sweet version of traditional risotto, using the main ingredients of apple and Arborio rice. The dish is a popular comfort food in Northern Italy, especially during the autumn season due to the seasonality of apples.

It pairs a unique blend of ingredients to produce a sweet and tart yet savoury dish. Also, the soft and chewy texture of the risotto paired with the softened apple cubes add a nice touch to this delicious meal.

Ingredients for 2 plates

  • 1/2 apples
  • 1 teaspoon of lemon juice
  • 1/4 of onion
  • 2 tablespoons of butter
  • 1/2 cup of risotto rice
  • 2 tablespoons of white wine
  • 1 cup of apple juice
  • 1 cup of water
  • 1 teaspoon of granulated consommé
  • Salt & Pepper
  • 4 slices of thick cut bacon
  • Black pepper (optional)
  • Powdered cheese (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the bacon in a frying pan until it's crispy and set to the side.
  2. Peel the apple and dice it in cubes of 1cm³.
  3. Mince the onion.
  4. Heat the butter in a frying pan and sauté the onions.
  5. Add the rice and cook until it's transparent.
  6. Add the white wine and cook it until the alcohol burns off.
  7. Mix the apple juice, water and consommé. Slowly add this 1/8 cup at a time simmering on low heat for 15 minutes (until all the liquid is absorbed).
  8. Add the apple dices and lemon juice and season it to taste with salt and pepper.
  9. Place on a plate and top it with the bacon.
  10. You can use black pepper or powdered cheese as topping.
